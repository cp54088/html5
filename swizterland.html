Travel Two: Switzerland


Bern

The city of Bern is absolutely splendid. You can see the old and new styles of architecture throughout the city. This capital city is built around the Aare River. You can spend time in interesting shops. we think you will enjoy this city.



Chateau de Chillon

This beautiful old castle is a great place to stop and explore. It is built on the water and has a spectacular view from inside. It's also a famous historical site that displays a medieval style. You should defnitely take time on your trip to see this castle.

For more information on this castle, click here.



Zurich

Zurich is such a magnificent city! It has all the charms of other cities, but so much more. You can shop in upscale neighborhoods and sample traditional foods. There are lots of museums and art galleries to visit too. There really is something for everyone in Zurich.



Geneva

Cassie and I loved Geneva. It is surrounded by mountain ranges and is on Geneva Lake. The water is a brilliant blue and looks amazing in photos. You can tour the cities busy streets and go see the headquarters of Europe's United Nations and Red Cross buildings. Don't forget to appreiate the French influence on the food and architecture when you go.



Swiss Alps

The Swiss Alps is the place to go when visiting Switzerland. The country is known for its beautiful lanscapes, especially the always snow-covered Alps. We couldn't stop taking pictures while mountain climbing. This picture we took shows one of the most famous mountain peaks, Matterhorn.



Zermatt

The air around Zermatt is chilly all year round! We had to bundle up while wandering through the towns stone streets. Cute little shops line many of the streets, making it fun to just wander around shopping. But down forget to visit the older areas, as they have a completely different feel.


Matterhorn Glacier Paradise

Inside the caves of Matterhorn peak is an amazing display of carved glaciers. There are winding tunnels that showcase different ice sculptures. The whole exhibit is dimly lit and allows the people strolling through to appreciate the detail and beauty.
